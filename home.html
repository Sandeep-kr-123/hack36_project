2). Competitive Exams


Introduction:

Competitive exams are the exams which are held on different modes on different aspects.
The judging criteria of these exams are based on the marks you score or based on the comparison of your marks with toppers marks and declaring percentage criteria.
Here we are going to discuss some of exams to make you familiar and well knowledged about different exams
Before moving ahead how much brilliant you are but this society doesn’t care about your intelligence if you fail to succeed in exams so here we are sharing details of some exams and tips and tricks and strategy to be followed to succeed.


1] JEE MAIN

About:- This is online mode examination which held every year to admit students to NIT’s and IIIT’s across the India for pursuing engineering. Conducted by NTA under MHRD.
Frequency:- 4 times a year (as per new rules) in February, March, April, May Eligibility: students who pursued intermediate or equal certificate before 2 years to students who are pursuing this year, no age limit.
Application:- Application to be done on online mode.
Subjects:- 5 subjects with 3 compulsory and 2 elective/optional
For B.tech :
Mathematics, Physics and one of (Chemistry/biotechnology/biology/technical vocational subjects) with 2 other electives.
For B.arch:
Mathematics with either of two subjects from (Chemistry/biotechnology/biology/technical vocational subjects) with 2 other electives.


2] JEE ADVANCED

About: This exam is set by IIT’s and IISC to give admission to students who are eligible in one of various engineering branches and also for B.Arch. and B. Planning. Every year one of the IIT take lead and conducts examination.
Frequency: Once a year.
Eligibility: One who qualified JEE mains and has a minimum of 75% score in board exam for general candidates and 65% for SC/ST and PWD
Application: Application can be done online guidelines are shared by NTA in score card of JEE main.
Subjects: Mathematics, Physics, chemistry.


3] GATE

About: GRADUATE APTITUDE TEST IN ENGINEERING is known as GATE is conducted by 7 IIT’s and IISC under MHRD to provide masters for the candidates who are eligible and the GATE score is considered by some of PSUs to offer jobs to candidates. It is a three hours exam.
Frequency: Once a year
Eligibility: Bachelor's degree holders in Engineering / Technology / Architecture (3 years after 10+2/ 10+2+3(ongoing)/ 10+2+4(ongoing)/ Post-B.Sc./ Post-Diploma) and those who are in the final year of such programs (Also prefinal year of B. tech).
• Master's degree holders in any branch of Science/Mathematics/Statistics/Computer Applications or equivalent and those who are in the final year of such programs.
• Candidates in the second or higher year of Four-year integrated master's degree programs (Post-B.Sc.) in Engineering / Technology.
• Candidates in the fourth or higher year of Five-year integrated master's degree programs or Dual Degree programs in Engineering / Technology.
• Candidates with qualifications obtained through examinations conducted by professional societies recognized by UGC/AICTE (e.g., AMIE by IE(India), AMICE by the Institute of Civil Engineers (India)-ICE(I), AMIETE by IETE(India)) as equivalent to B.E./B.Tech.
Those who have completed section A or equivalent of such professional courses are also eligible. There is no age limit criterion defined by the exam conducting authority to appear in GATE.
Application: The application is to be done on online mode. Guidelines are provided by GATE board.
Subjects: Verbal ability, Numerical ability, Engineering mathematics, Technical ability.


4] SBI PO

ABOUT:- This exam is conducted by State Bank of India. It is conducted to select candidates for the post of Probationary Officers (PO).
Application Mode: - Online

3). ACADEMICAL EDUCATION

Introduction:

Many of the parents have a doubt that on what stream their children should be educated, so in this section we discussed briefly about academic education and by that we hope one can get a clarity to where and on what platform to make their children study. Academic education is the prime thing for the better and smooth foundation of life. It tells the whole personality of a person on a boarder scale. From centuries, the main focus of humans is to gather better and quality academic education. Academic education provides a certification for being literate from social class. In most cases, people who are better in academics in academic, come up with flying colors in their personal and professional life. Education system in India is upgrading day by day, to meet the needs of present. With increased number of boards and institutes, it become difficult to chose the better one to help you with this, we have collected information on various boards that are trending in this decade. From this you will get a clear picture on all the major boards in India.


1] CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education):

This is the most popular board in India which is under the control of central government of India. It’s all methodologies and contents are approved by central government.
The content of this board primarily focuses on covering the syllabus of prestigious exams like JEE and NEET. Mostly the content of these exams revolves around the books provided by this board.
The two major exams under this board happens in class10 (Highschool) and class12 (Intermediate).


2] ICSE/ISC (INDIAN CERTIFICATE OF SECONDARY EDUCATION)

ICSE has also expanded its wings all over the nation. It is a national board but is held privately. This board aims to provide high quality education to its students. Its syllabus is different in many aspects in comparison to CBSE one.
This board focuses on overall development of student by emphasizing on all areas and subjects with equal and utmost importance. Also this board focuses more on English language understanding and speaking. This board conducts three examinations namely:-
-->ICSE – CLASS X
-->ISC – CLASS XII
-->CVE – Year 12

3] State boards

Every state in India has its specific state board to encourage the local language and principles among their tiny toddlers. There are more than 30 state boards in India which are successfully providing education to their citizens.
State board focus on all the levels of society, specially the one’s who don’t have enough resources for quality education. State board provide various incentives from time to time to the weaker and needy section of society.
All the rules and regulations in state board run schools are advised from state board only.

When class 12 ends with protective and safer environment of school and makes the students ready to step in their college life. The carrier opportunities that can be chosen after completing 12 mainly depends on the stream chosen. A brief idea on carrier graduation opportunities or different stream is provided here: -


For BiPC students

1] MBBS (Bachelor of medical and bachelor of surgery)

It 5 and half year long course intended to become a certified doctor in India. It consists of 1 year compulsory internship under experienced doctor. The most compulsory internship under experienced doctor. The most common competitive exam to get into this is NEET


2] BDS (Bachelor of dental surgery)

This is a 5-year long course consisting of compulsory 1 year internship. This aim for dental doctor in India and has vast scope in governmental and private sector.


3] BSC (Bachelor of science)

This can be done from choosing subject from a variety of options like. Botany, Biochemistry, ecology, environmental science, molecular biology, physics, zoology.
This course has a duration of three-years, different colleges have their own way of conducting their own entrance examination for this course, they are eligible for doing jobs in not only their course related but also in teaching, banking etc.….


FOR MPC STUDENTS:


1] ENGINEERING (B. tech/BE):

India is very popular in terms of its vast number of engineers and engineering institutes. This is the most famous course done by MPC students in India. The most prestigious exam in India is JEE mains and advanced on basis of the ranks they obtained in this exam they are allotted seats in various prestigious colleges in India like NIT’s, IIT’s, IIIT’s and GFTI’s in various branches of engineering.


2]BCA (Bachelor of computer applications)

With the advancements in latest technology computers gained most importance and this course became equivalent to b. tech in computer science. The duration of course is three years. They can even pursue a further degree that is MCA or they can get job in IT company on basis of this.


FOR COMMERCE STUDENTS


1] B. COM (Bachelor in commerce)

This course for commerce stream students has a duration of 3 years. This course paves the path for making career opportunity in field of banking, accountancy, economist etc.…. This course suits best for students who want to pursue carrier in financial or business-related fields.


2] CA (Charted Accountancy)

The CA’s are estimated to be the most successful financials of a country. Entrance exams are conducted to admit students for CA study. This course has a duration of 5 years approximately, This course doesn’t have any age limit to stop you but only and the barrier you have to cross is the exam ICAI.


FOR ART’S/HUMANITIES STUDENTS


1] BA-LLB (Bachelor of arts and bachelor of law)

This is a graduation course for law interested aspirants. This course has a duration of 5 years. This course can be understood as sum of arts and law. Admission under this course is done through CLAT examination.


2] BFA (Bachelor of Fine Arts)
